## What is a study path

A **study path** is not browsing the site at random or doing every tutorial without context. It is the recommended order of the **Microcontrollers** course (UNEXPO · Mechatronics): understand the concept, apply it with a tutorial, then prepare for the partial exam.

The flow has clear phases:

1. **Getting started** — microcontroller concepts, what the PIC18F4550 is, and comparison with other platforms.
2. **Foundations** — architecture, registers, assembly, and your first program: MPLAB, ports guide, and First LED tutorial.
3. **Course** — peripherals with guides and UNEXPO labs interleaved where they belong.
4. **Partials** — review and interactive exam right after the covered topics.

## Tutorials on the path

<Aside type="tip" title="Class, guide, and lab in the same order">
The list below deliberately mixes three lesson types:

- **Class** — course theory: registers, concepts, diagrams.
- **Practical guide** — short steps to practice a skill before the official lab.
- **Lab** — UNEXPO practice with code, Proteus, design challenge, and exam.

Do not jump to the [tutorial catalog](/en/tutoriales/) without reading the prior theory. On the path, each tutorial comes **right after** what prepares you: after assembly and MPLAB, First LED; later, after interrupts, the interrupts guide and then the lab.
</Aside>

## Golden rule

Do not memorize full tables at first. The key exam skill is answering:

**What do I want to control → which register configures it → which bit must I modify**
